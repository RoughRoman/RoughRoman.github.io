### Overview AWS global infrastructure is made up of a few concepts * AWS Regions: * AWS has regions all around the world. They have names like us-east-1 af-south-1 etc * A region is a cluster of data centres * Most AWS services are region-scoped * How do you choose an AWS region? * It depends, but here are some factors * Compliance with data governance and legal requirements * Proximity to customers: reduced latency * Available services within a region. Not all services are available in all regions. * Pricing: pricing varies region to region * AWS Availability Zones * Each Region has many availability zones, usually 3, min is 3 max is 6 * Example: Region = ap-southeast-2 | 3 availability zones = ap-southeast-2a, ap-southeast-2b, ap-southeast-2c * Each of these zones are one or more discrete data centres with redundant power, networking and connectivity. * They are separate from each other so they are isolated from disasters * They are connected with high bandwidth, ultra low latency networks. * AWS points of presence(Edge locations) * Amazon has 400+ points of presence. content is delivered to end users with lower latency. https://aws.amazon.com/about-aws/global-infrastructure/regions_az/ ### Services - Route 53 - CloudFront (CDN) - S3 Transfer ### Further Notes - Why make a global Application #global-infrastructure #introduction
